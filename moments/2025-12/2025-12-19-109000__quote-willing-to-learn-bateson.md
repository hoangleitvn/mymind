# We Are Not What We Know, But What We Are Willing to Learn

## The Quote
> "We are not what we know but what we are willing to learn."

## Attribution
**Mary Catherine Bateson** (1939-2021)
- American writer and cultural anthropologist
- Daughter of Margaret Mead and Gregory Bateson
- Author of "Composing a Life" and "Willing to Learn: Passages of Personal Discovery" (2004)

## Why It Resonates
- Identity is defined by openness, not accumulated knowledge
- Growth mindset articulated before the term was popularized
- Challenges the "expert" identity trap
- Relevant in the AI era where knowledge is commoditized but curiosity isn't

## Context
From her 2004 book "Willing to Learn: Passages of Personal Discovery"

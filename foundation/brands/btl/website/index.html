What is Builds That Last?

I've been building and leading engineering teams for 15 years. Over 50 projects. Startups, enterprises, legacy systems, greenfield builds. Some still live. Some failed. Some never shipped.

And I keep seeing the same pattern. Teams ship fast. Then they slow down. Not because the engineers got lazy. Because the foundation was never there.

This publication is for engineers and founders who want to build things that last, not things that break. Real lessons from real projects. What works, what fails, and why foundation matters more than ever in the AI era.

Philosophy

Five Principles

These principles guide how we think about building software that lasts. They're not rules to follow blindly, but lenses to see through.

01

Depth Over Surface

What you see should suggest what's beneath. The demo works, but what comes after separates software that lasts from software that collapses.

02

Clarity Over Cleverness

Understanding beats impressiveness. Code that humans can maintain is worth more than code that machines can generate.

03

Restraint Over Abundance

Less, but intentional. Every feature, every abstraction, every dependency should earn its place in the system.

04

Grounded Over Floating

Weight and stability over lightness. Systems need foundations that anchor them when reality gets complicated.

05

Timeless Over Trendy

Built to last, not built for now. The fundamentals matter more than ever when tools change every quarter.

Featured Article

Why Foundation Comes Before Speed

What you see is 20% above the water. The shiny demos. The fast shipping. What you don't see is the 80% below. The maintenance. The technical debt. Engineers connecting systems that were never meant to work together.

"The demo works. The demo always works. It's what comes after that separates software that lasts from software that collapses."

This is the iceberg problem. People say "build the 20% that matters." But what about the trade-offs and risks that come with the other 80%? Nobody talks about that part.

## Original Post

Comments are doing more work on LinkedIn right now than most people's posts. I've had comments that hit 30K impressions in less than a day (see pic as proof). With my regular posts, I'm lucky if I hit 2,000. To quote Jessica Aries, who inspired me to write this post, "Comments are not an afterthought. They are content." But they also serve a purpose in increasing the reach of your own LinkedIn posts. I've talked before about how LinkedIn rolled out a new AI brain for ranking and recommendations in 2025. The biggest shift: the algorithm no longer prioritizes content based on who you know (your connections) but on what you care about (your topics). This transition means your feed is now populated by AI-driven interest clusters that serve content based on your specific engagement history and professional relevance, regardless of whether you are connected to the creator. What does that have to do with comments? Every comment you write is logged as prompt data that trains the algorithm on your expertise. When you leave a comment, you provide a real-time example that helps the system predict that you (and people like you) should see similar content in the future. The algorithm also rewards reciprocity. If you thoughtfully engage with a prospect or peer, the system increases the probability of pulling your next post into their feed.

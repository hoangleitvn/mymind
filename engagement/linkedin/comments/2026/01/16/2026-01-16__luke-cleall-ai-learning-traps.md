## Original Post

I wasted 6 months learning AI the wrong way. Watched every tutorial. Studied every workflow. Followed every guru. "Build this 47-step automation!" "This workflow saves 200 hours a month!" "Here's my $10K/month AI system!" I saved the videos. Took notes. Felt like I was making progress. I wasn't. I was just procrastinating with a purpose. 6 months of learning. Zero value provided. And here's why it's so easy to get stuck there: Your brain doesn't want you to take risks. It wants you to stay safe. Watching tutorials feels productive. Building something that might fail? That's uncomfortable. So we keep learning. Keep preparing. Keep waiting until we're "ready." We never are. When I finally started building, I made the opposite mistake. Built workflows that looked impressive. Multiple modules. Clever logic. Some never got used. They solved problems nobody actually had. I was copying what looked good on YouTube instead of solving what was painful in real life. ùóßùóøùóÆùóΩ ùü≠: Learning from content designed to impress, not help. ùóßùóøùóÆùóΩ ùüÆ: Building solutions before understanding pain points. Both feel safe. Neither move the needle. The gurus aren't lying. But they're optimising for views, not your results. Your business doesn't need a 47-step workflow. It needs the simple automations that move data, complete quotes, generate replies, etc. The only thing that changes anything? Actually starting. Messy. Imperfect. Probably broken the first time. That's how you learn. Not by watching someone else do it. Which traps have you fallen into?

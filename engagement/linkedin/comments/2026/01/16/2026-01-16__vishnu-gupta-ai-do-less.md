## Original Post

Most people use AI to do more. More messages. More content. More workflows. More noise. I use AI for the opposite. To do less. Less chasing. Less reminding. Less manual follow-ups. Less context switching. The biggest shift for me wasn't adding new tools. It was deciding what should never need my attention again. Here's the rule I follow: If a task – repeats every week – follows the same pattern – doesn't require judgment it shouldn't live in my head. It should live in a system. That's where tools like n8n actually shine. Not in flashy demos. In boring reliability. AI doesn't create leverage by being smart. It creates leverage by being consistent. Humans are creative. Systems are disciplined. When each does its job, things start compounding quietly. That's the part most people miss. What's one task you're still carrying in your head that should already be handled by a system?

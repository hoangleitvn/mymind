## Original Post

Everyone's talking about agents. Almost no one should be building them yet. The hype is real. The technology is coming. But 90% of executives claim they're confident making AI decisions while only 8% actually possess substantial AI knowledge. That 82-point gap is filled with buzzwords. Agent. Agentic. Automation. Most people think they're the same thing. They're not. An agent operates autonomously. Perceiving its environment, making rational decisions, pursuing goals without constant oversight. What most companies need? Automation. Rules-based workflows that run the same way every time. Not sexy. But it works. And here's the thing: if you can't automate your processes effectively, you're not ready for agentic systems. And you're definitely not ready for true agents. The foundation has to come first. Honest question: Do you know the difference between automation, agentic systems, and agents? Or does it all blur together?

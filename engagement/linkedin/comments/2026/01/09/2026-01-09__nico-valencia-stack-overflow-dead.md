## Original Post

Stack Overflow is dead. No one bothers to post solutions anymore.

Developers encounter errors, research yields nothing (platforms like Stack Overflow are "dead"), and they manually debug in isolation. "A LOT of time is spent here, and more developers are feeling this in 2026."

Rather than solutions entering the public domain through SEO, they're captured in "foundation lab databases." These companies are "watching developers solve these problems" and will eventually monetize this data by reselling solutions back to developers.

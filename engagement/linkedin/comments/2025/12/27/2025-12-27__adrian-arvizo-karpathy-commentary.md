## Original Post

Andrej Karpathy just described what every experienced technologist is quietly feeling: the ground is shifting, and there's no manual.

For those unfamiliar: Karpathy was Director of AI at Tesla leading Autopilot, a founding member of OpenAI, and created Stanford's most popular deep learning course. When he says he's never felt this far behind as a programmer, it's worth paying attention.

The new "programmable layer" he describes, with agents, prompts, memory systems, MCP integrations, isn't just another framework to learn. It's a fundamentally different kind of engineering where the components are stochastic and the failure modes are unpredictable.

AI tooling demands a paradigm shift. You're not debugging deterministic code anymore. You're orchestrating probabilistic systems while maintaining enough structure to ship reliable products.

The "10X productivity" Karpathy mentions is real. So is the learning curve to claim it.

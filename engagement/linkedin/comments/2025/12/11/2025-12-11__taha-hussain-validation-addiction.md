## Original Post

Most people say they are afraid to post. I do not believe them. They are afraid of what happens next. No likes? They spiral. No comments? They rewrite their personality. One rude reply? They vanish for six weeks. I know this pattern because I have lived inside it. I used to write a post, feel a little spark, then kill it with questions: "Will people like this?" "What if it flops?" "Let me run this by 4 friends before I share it." By the time the fourth friend replied with a thumbs up, the post was dead. Not because it was bad. Because I had handed my courage to a group chat. That is not a content problem. That is a validation addiction problem. Creative work suffocates when its worth is measured in applause. Here is what I started doing instead: I post like I am the only one watching. I write like no one is reading yet. I build like my life in one year depends on it. Sometimes it is quiet. Sometimes it flops. Sometimes it stings. But something strange happens when you keep publishing in the quiet. You stop refreshing the numbers. You start trusting your own taste. You begin to sound like yourself. Applause is not strategy. Attention is not leverage. Validation is not currency. The real power is in publishing when it is silent. Because when it finally gets loud? You will be the only one who knows you earned it.

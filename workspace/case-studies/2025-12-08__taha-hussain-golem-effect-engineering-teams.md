# The Golem Effect in Engineering Teams

**Author:** Taha Hussain
**Source:** [Substack Article](https://tahahussain.substack.com/p/the-golem-effect-in-engineering-teams)
**Date Captured:** 2025-12-08

## Summary

The Golem Effect describes how low expectations from leaders quietly undermine employee performance. Unlike the Pygmalion Effect (high expectations boost achievement), this pattern operates in reverse: managers inadvertently construct performance limitations through subtle behavioral choices.

## Core Concept

Leaders create underperformers through unconscious behavioral patterns that signal doubt in capabilities. This happens through four "leakage channels" where expectations manifest.

## The Manager's Unconscious Sabotage

Hussain illustrates how his protective instincts toward a newly hired bootcamp graduate actually stunted her growth. By keeping her off on-call rotations, high-stakes projects, and visibility opportunities, he signaled fundamental doubt in her capabilities.

**The engineer's response:** "If you never give me things that matter, how am I supposed to prove I matter?"

## Four Leakage Channels

| Channel | Description | Low Expectation Signal |
|---------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Climate** | Emotional temperature differs by person | Some get full attention; others get distracted engagement |
| **Input** | Strategic context distribution | Perceived stars get context; others receive only task descriptions |
| **Output** | Work assignment patterns | Favored members handle visible, important work; others get background tasks |
| **Feedback** | Quality of guidance | High-potential get specific growth pathways; others get generic praise |

## Key Insight

> "Low expectations don't stay safely inside your head. They leak into your calendar, your tone, and your assignments" through consistent behavioral patterns that compound over time.

## Remedies

1. **Engineer early wins with real impact** - Build genuine confidence through meaningful work
2. **Replace limiting labels with gap-focused language** - Focus on skills to develop, not fixed assessments
3. **Resist taking over when team members struggle** - Maintain proximity, not control
4. **Conduct weekly audits** - Track whose work gets visibility and whose feedback includes growth direction

## Application to Engineering Leadership

This framework is relevant for:
- Engineering managers assessing team performance
- CTOs building team culture
- Tech leads mentoring junior developers
- Anyone responsible for engineer growth and development

## Related Concepts

- **Pygmalion Effect** (opposite) - High expectations boost achievement
- **Self-fulfilling prophecy** - Expectations shape outcomes
- **Psychological safety** - Creating environments where people can take risks

## My Takeaways

1. Audit where my calendar time goes - this reveals real expectations
2. Check the four leakage channels quarterly for each team member
3. Watch for "protective" instincts that actually limit growth
4. Ensure junior engineers get context, not just tickets

You're on a mountain. Heavy rain starts.

Do you go up or down?

I asked my team this question yesterday. Both said: "Down. Get off the mountain."

Wrong answer.

You go UP.

Why? Flash floods. Rain collects in valleys. The path down becomes a river. People die every year making this mistake.

The intuitive answer kills you. The counterintuitive answer saves you.

This is reverse thinking. And it applies to work too.

Intuitive: Fix the bug in front of you.
Reverse: Is this bug the most important thing right now?

Intuitive: Ship this feature fast.
Reverse: What breaks if we ship it incomplete?

Intuitive: Say yes to the client request.
Reverse: What are we saying no to by saying yes?

Most people follow intuition.
Good decision-makers think through consequences.

Before your next decision, ask:

"What does my intuition say? Now, what happens if I do the opposite?"

Sometimes the opposite is the answer.

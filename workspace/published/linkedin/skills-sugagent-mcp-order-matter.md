Everyone talks about MCP, Subagents, and Skills.

But the more I work with AI coding assistants, the more I think **Skills are the foundation** we should focus on first.

Here's my thinking:

**Skills = Knowledge base**
- Your architecture patterns
- Engineering principles
- Code standards
- System structure
- Mental models
- and soft skills

When the AI understands these, it doesn't just write code. It makes decisions aligned with how you think.

**Skills help with more than coding**
- Problem-solving approaches
- Decision-making frameworks
- Trade-off evaluation

Once you have strong Skills in place, then workflow and process (Subagents) make sense. Then external integrations (MCP) when you truly need them.

**My current approach:**

I'm spending significant time right now building my knowledge base. Codifying my architecture principles. Structuring my Skills. Teaching the AI how I think, not just what I need.

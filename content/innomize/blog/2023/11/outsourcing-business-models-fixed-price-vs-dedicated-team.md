# Outsourcing Business Models: Fixed Price VS. Dedicated Team

There are two common outsourcing business models to consider when you are working with an IT offshore partner : Fixed Price and Dedicated Team. Understanding the benefits and challenges associated with each model is crucial for companies aiming to optimize their outsourcing decisions in software development.

## Outsourcing Business Models : Fixed Price VS. Dedicated Team

 ![](https://res.cloudinary.com/innomizetech/image/upload/v1695288376/production/teamwork_1642d2cdb5.jpg) 

### **Fixed Price Model**

The fixed price outsourcing model is a well-defined approach where the service provider and the client agree on a predetermined project scope and a fixed budget. This model is ideal for projects with clear and stable requirements, as the scope of work is agreed upon before the project begins. 

This engagement model is optimal for businesses seeking specialized skills for short-term initiatives, allowing for flexibility in managing resources and costs. Fixed price model offers distinct advantages, including cost-effectiveness by paying for project-specific tasks and access to diverse skill sets that might not be available in-house. Moreover, it facilitates scalability, enabling companies to adjust outsourcing needs based on project requirements.

However, challenges can arise in terms of aligning the outsourcing provider's deliverables with the client's long-term software development goals. Communication gaps and potential inconsistencies may emerge, particularly if project specifications evolve during the development process, necessitating a robust communication strategy and meticulous project management to ensure successful project delivery.

Changes in client requirements can lead to scope creep, where the project's scope expands beyond the initial agreement.
Clearly define the project scope in the initial contract and document any changes requested by the client. Implement a formal change management process where any additional work is documented, approved, and added to the project scope with corresponding adjustments in timeline and budget.

### **Dedicated Team Model**

 ![](https://res.cloudinary.com/innomizetech/image/upload/v1688122329/production/dedicated_team_process_d6c04bea4e.png) 

Conversely, the dedicated team model involves establishing a long-term partnership with an external team dedicated exclusively to the client's software development projects. This model is advantageous for businesses requiring continuous support, ongoing software development, and maintenance. By integrating seamlessly with the client's internal processes, this business model fosters a sense of ownership and accountability, aligning the external team's efforts with the client's overarching business objectives.

Dedicated teams offer a collaborative environment, allowing for real-time adjustments, streamlined communication, and enhanced productivity. The increased control over the project's direction enables businesses to make necessary modifications promptly, resulting in improved efficiency and reduced time-to-market for software products.

However, initiating a dedicated team outsourcing model may require a more substantial initial investment compared to the project-based process. Establishing effective communication channels, fostering a cohesive team culture, and maintaining alignment with the client's software development vision are critical components for ensuring the success of a dedicated team engagement.

### **Choosing the Ideal Business Model**

Selecting the most suitable outsourcing engagement model depends on the specific requirements, scope, and duration of the project. For short-term and well-defined software development projects, the project-based model is cost-effective and flexible. In contrast, the dedicated team model is preferable for long-term projects demanding continuous development and maintenance, fostering a collaborative and integrated software development process.

ðŸ‘‰ Project Complexity: For simple and well-defined projects, a fixed price model might be suitable. However, for complex, long-term initiatives with evolving requirements, a dedicated team model offers the flexibility needed for success.

ðŸ‘‰ Budget Constraints: If you have a strict budget and clear project requirements, a fixed price model provides cost predictability. Alternatively, if you can allocate a larger budget for ongoing development and collaboration, a dedicated team might be the better choice.

ðŸ‘‰ Long-term Goals: Consider your long-term business objectives. If you anticipate the need for ongoing development, maintenance, and support, a dedicated team can provide continuity and consistent quality over time.

ðŸ‘‰ Communication and Collaboration: Evaluate your communication preferences and the level of collaboration required. If you prefer direct involvement and seamless integration with your team, a dedicated team model is the way to go.

## Conclusion

In the IT industry, both project-based and dedicated team models offer unique benefits and challenges. Businesses must meticulously assess their software development requirements, budget constraints, and long-term objectives to determine the most appropriate outsourcing model. Effective communication, strategic planning, and fostering a collaborative work environment are imperative for ensuring successful outcomes in software project development outsourcing engagements. By aligning the chosen model with their unique business needs, companies can achieve greater efficiency, improved productivity, and accelerated growth in the competitive software development market.

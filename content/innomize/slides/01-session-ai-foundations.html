What is Generative AI?

AI that generates new content based on patterns learned from training data.

What It Can Generate

  • Text (articles, code, emails)
  • Images (from descriptions)
  • Audio (voice, music)
  • Video (emerging)
  • Structured data (tables, JSON)

Popular Tools

  • Text: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini
  • Code: Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code
  • Images: DALL-E, Midjourney
  • Audio: ElevenLabs, Whisper

Known Issues & Limitations

Hallucinations

AI confidently generates false information. It doesn't know what it doesn't know.

Always verify facts, numbers, and references.

Context Limits

Each model has a maximum "window" of text it can process. Loses track in long conversations.

Break long tasks into smaller sessions.

No Real-Time Knowledge

Training data has a cutoff date. Doesn't know recent events or your company specifics.

Provide context. Don't assume AI knows.

Bias & Inconsistency

Reflects biases in training data. Same prompt can give different results.

Review outputs critically. Test multiple times.

AI Adoption vs AI/ML Creation

Two different paths. Most organizations need adoption, not creation.

AI Adoption AI/ML Creation
What Use existing AI tools (GPT, Claude, Gemini) Build custom models, train on your data
Skills needed Workflow design, validation, prompting ML engineering, data science, infrastructure
Investment Low-medium (subscriptions, training) High (team, compute, data, time)
Time to value Days to weeks Months to years
Who needs this Most companies (90%+) Companies with unique data/problems

How AI Actually Works

INNOMIZE

AI is Pattern Matching, Not Thinking

AI was trained on massive amounts of text and images.

It learned patterns: "when I see X, Y usually comes next."

AI predicts "what comes next" based on patterns it has seen before.

This is powerful, but it's not understanding. It's sophisticated pattern matching.

9
INNOMIZE

What This Means in Practice

  • No memory between sessions — Each conversation starts fresh (unless you build context)
  • Confident but not always correct — AI doesn't know what it doesn't know
  • Pattern-dependent — Works well for common patterns, struggles with unusual cases
  • No real-world knowledge after training — Can't know recent events, your company, your context

Key Insight

AI is a tool that extends your capability. It doesn't replace your judgment.

Two Types of AI Models

Generative Models Reasoning Models
Examples GPT-4, Gemini, Claude (standard) Claude with thinking, o1, o3
Speed Fast Slower (thinking takes time)
Style Creative, broad Deliberate, logical
Best for Drafting, brainstorming Complex problems, analysis

INNOMIZE

What Each Model Needs From You

Generative Models

Need HOW — Instructions

  • Step-by-step guidance
  • Clear format requirements
  • Examples of what you want
  • Specific constraints

Reasoning Models

Need WHAT + WHY — Context

  • The problem to solve
  • Why it matters
  • Success criteria
  • Constraints and context

Part 3

What AI Is Best Suited For

15
INNOMIZE

AI Excels At

  • First drafts — Content, code, documents
  • Summarization — Long docs to key points
  • Translation — Languages, formats
  • Brainstorming — Ideas, options, variations
  • Explanation — Make complex things simple
  • Pattern tasks — Formatting, extraction
  • Tedious work — Repetitive but clear tasks
  • Learning — Explain concepts, answer questions

The Pattern

AI is best when the task is clear, the pattern is common, and perfect accuracy isn't required.

16
INNOMIZE

AI Struggles With

  • Factual accuracy — Especially Vietnam-specific policy and law
  • Nuanced images — The specific vision in your head
  • Long context memory — Loses track in long conversations
  • Vietnamese language nuance — Improving, but not perfect
  • Judgment calls — Decisions requiring domain expertise
  • Knowing what it doesn't know — Will guess confidently

Your Challenge

Vietnam policy/law requires human verification. AI can help search and summarize, but you must validate against official sources.

17
INNOMIZE

The Simple Rule

Use AI for the first 80%.
Use your expertise for the final 20%.

AI generates the draft. You verify, correct, and finalize.

AI saves time on the tedious parts. You add the judgment and accuracy.

Key Takeaways

1. AI is a tool, not a replacement

It extends your capability. It doesn't replace your judgment.

2. Match the model to the task

Generative for drafts and creativity. Reasoning for complex analysis.

3. AI is best for first drafts and tedious work

Let AI do the 80%. You verify and finalize the 20%.

4. Always validate, especially for facts

Vietnam policy, numbers, dates — verify against official sources.

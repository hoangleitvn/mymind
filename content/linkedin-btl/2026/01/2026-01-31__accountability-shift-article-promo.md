Issue #4 of Builds That Last is live.

Everyone ships fast now. The question changed.

We wrote about what that new question is in our latest article: "The Accountability Shift."

After observing teams using AI for the past year, patterns emerged:

The Verification Gap
→ Code ships without deep review because "it looks right."

The Understanding Gap
→ Code ships that the person who shipped it can't fully explain.

The Ownership Gap
→ Engineers wait for tickets instead of proposing solutions.

Three types of debt now compound faster than ever:
→ Verification debt (no system to catch errors)
→ Quality debt (architectural accidents from AI-generated patterns)
→ Foundation debt (context that walks out the door)

The shift teams need:
→ Speed to clarity
→ Habitual to intentional
→ Waiting to owning

Read the full article: https://buildsthatlast.substack.com/p/the-accountability-shift

In Software 2.0, the question isn't "how fast can you ship?"

It's "who's accountable when things break?"

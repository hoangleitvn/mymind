Stop waiting for someone to promote you to architect.

Nobody's coming. The mindset promotes itself.

I've seen engineers wait years for a title that never came. They thought architecture was reserved for senior people. For those with decades of experience. For the ones in the room with the whiteboard.

That's backwards.

Architecture is a mindset. You either have it or you don't.

Here are 10 signs you already think like an architect:

1/ You ask "why" before "how"
↳ Problems first. Tech stack later.

2/ You think about who maintains it
↳ Building is easy. Living with it is hard.

3/ You sketch before you code
↳ Cheap mistakes on paper. Expensive ones in production.

4/ You ask about constraints before requirements
↳ Budget, timeline, team — these shape everything.

5/ You know when to design and when to ship
↳ Not everything needs a blueprint. Not everything should skip one.

6/ You explain tradeoffs, not just decisions
↳ "We chose X because Y" beats "we chose X."

7/ You consider cost before scale
↳ Scale you don't need is waste you pay for.

8/ "Good enough" is a feature
↳ Perfection is a trap. Shipping is a skill.

9/ You've killed your own ideas
↳ Evidence beats ego. Every time.

10/ You build frameworks, not just features
↳ Patterns scale. One-offs don't.

The title comes later. The mindset comes first.

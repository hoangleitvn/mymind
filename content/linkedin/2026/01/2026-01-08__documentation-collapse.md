StackOverflow used to see hundreds of thousands of questions every month at its peak.
Today, activity is a fraction of that.

Developers did not stop learning.
They stopped asking publicly. They ask AI instead.

Tooling companies built on documentation and reuse are under pressure.
Frameworks that once sold convenience now compete with prompts.

Two shifts. Same pattern.

Documentation is no longer the destination.
Frameworks are no longer the product.

Here is the uncomfortable question.

If human knowledge sharing slows down, what trains the next generation of models?

We trained AI on human discussion, examples, arguments, mistakes.
Now humans are sharing less. Or sharing privately with machines.

If that continues, models plateau.

This is the real build vs buy debate.
